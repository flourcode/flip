How to Use the Flipulator

1. The Property üè†

  • Purchase Price: The amount you will pay to buy the property.
  • After Repair Value (ARV): What the property will be worth after renovations. Pro Tip: This is the most important number to get right. Ask a real estate agent for "comps" (comparable recently sold properties) and be conservative with your estimate.

2. The Costs üõ†Ô∏è

  • Rehab & Repairs: The total cost of all labor and materials for the renovation. Pro Tip: Get detailed quotes from contractors and always add a 15-20% contingency fund for unexpected issues.
  • Total Transaction (%): Covers all closing costs for both buying and selling (realtor fees, title insurance, taxes). Pro Tip: 10% is a safe estimate. The biggest cost is usually the 5-6% real estate agent commission on the final sale.

3. The Money üí∞

  • Down (%): The percentage of the purchase price you pay in cash. This depends on your loan type; hard money loans often require 10-25%.
  • Rate (%): The annual interest rate for your loan. Hard money loans for flips are typically much higher (12-18%) than standard mortgages.
  • Hold (Mo): The total project duration in months, from buying to selling. Pro Tip: Be realistic. A typical timeline is 1 month to buy, 3-4 months to renovate, and 2-3 months to sell. Always add a buffer for delays.
  • Monthly ($): Your recurring carrying costs, NOT including the loan. This covers property taxes, insurance, and utilities. Pro Tip: Don't forget this! These costs directly eat into your profit every month you own the property.

Adapting for Multi-Unit Flips üè¢

Flipping a duplex, triplex, or small apartment building? Use the "Multi-Unit" toggle in the calculator. Here's how to adjust your numbers:

  • ARV: Enter the total After Repair Value for the entire building. This might be based on what a similar, renovated multi-unit building sold for, or the sum of what each unit could sell for individually as a condo.
  • Rehab & Repairs: Your costs will be higher. You're renovating multiple kitchens and bathrooms. However, costs for shared systems (like a roof or boiler) are spread across all units, which can be more efficient.
  • Monthly ($) - The Big Advantage: If the property has tenants, you can collect rent during the renovation! **The calculator automatically subtracts the rental income you enter from your monthly costs,** showing you how income can offset your holding expenses.
  • Financing: Loans for multi-unit properties (5+ units) are often commercial loans, which may require a larger down payment (25-30%). You can adjust the "Down %" field to reflect this.

  • Cash Needed: Down Payment + Rehab Costs + Upfront Transaction Costs + Total Holding Costs
